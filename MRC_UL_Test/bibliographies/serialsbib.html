    • "Dressed for adventure: working women and silent movie serials in the 1910s." ~*OPENBRACKET*~i>Feminist Studies~*OPENBRACKET*~/i> 21.n1 (Spring 1995): 67(24).

    • "Female adventure serials in newspapers, magazines and film, for and about working-class women, became immensely popular from the early 1910s to the early 1920s. These serials, which recounted stories of working-class women who prevailed over adversities and earned promotion and respect, were the fulfillment of working-class women's innermost desire for dignity, respect and power. That working-class women identified with these heroines is proven by the serials' popularity." [Expanded Academic Index]
