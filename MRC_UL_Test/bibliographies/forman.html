  • Hollinger, Karen.

    • "Losing the feminist drift: adaptations of "Les Liaisons dangereuses." (International Cinema)." Literature-Film Quarterly 24.n3 (July 1996): 293(8).
    • Full-text of this article/review is available for UC users only
    • "Recent film adaptations of Choderlos de Laclos's "Les Liaisons dangereuses" failed to address the feminist potential of the 1782 novel. Milos Forman's "Valmont" and Stephen Frears's "Dangerous Liaisons" do not focus on the motivations of Madame de Merteuil the way the novel does. The novel portrays her as rejecting the limited roles available to women in 18th century French society. Frears portrays her as more villainous and Valmont as more sympathetic, and his film focuses on romantic love more than sexual politics. Forman de-emphasizes the Merteuil character, focusing more on Valmont." [Expanded Academic Index]
